Display & Color ▾

How non-detect (U-qualified) results are substituted when computing Hazard Index, the IDW/HI surfaces, and stats. Per-analyte ROD exceedance flags are not affected — they always require an actual detection above ROD.

Layers ▾

Smooth kernel-density heat of samples that exceed ROD only — below-threshold points contribute nothing, so clean zones stay transparent. Warmer = larger exceedance / denser hot spots.

HI = Hg/204 + As/6.1 + Sb/51 + Tl/1.3. HI ≥ 1 means combined ROD exceedance (EPA RAGS). Categorically coloured by RAGS bins, clipped to the convex hull of sampled points.

Decision Units ▾

No DUs yet — click "+ New DU" above, or draw a shape and use "Mark as DU" in its popup.

Scenarios ▾

Switching the active scenario shows only that scenario's DUs on the map. The "Unassigned" scenario always exists and holds any DU whose scenario was deleted.

Compare scenarios ▾

Full stats behind each DU's PASS/ACT call — mean, max, UCL-t, UCL-C, %>ROD, %>PMB for every analyte, plus depth, volume, Hg mass, and the call under both standards.

Hot-spot DU generator ▾

Runs the IDW delineation at the chosen Hg threshold and converts each contour polygon into a DU (action = Hand-excavate hot spot) assigned to the active scenario.

DU settings ▾

Switches the PASS/ACT call on every DU. Use Background if EPA treats the ABP as an accessible area requiring restoration to natural levels; use ROD if it falls under the mine-area standard. Both shows two badges so you can present the picture under each.

UCL method. Per-DU stats show Student-t UCL and Chebyshev UCL on raw values (½ DL substitution for non-detects). PASS/ACT uses the Chebyshev (conservative).
Screening only. Formal compliance is per Exposure Unit via ProUCL. Per-DU UCL is a screening aid, not a compliance test.
Sb & Tl. When 100% of in-DU samples are non-detect, the report shows "All ND at MDL X, < ROD Y — no further action indicated" instead of stats.
